<TLDR>
The handmade web is the opposite of the templated, rented, generic web: sites built by people, for specific people, with voice, weight, and texture you can feel. Ten short credos follow, arguing that craft is the thing visitors notice without being able to name, and that the websites worth making are the ones that feel a little bit alive.
</TLDR>

Somewhere along the way, websites started feeling the same. Not bad. Just the same. Stock hero, three-up grid, accent colour on the CTA, testimonial carousel, footer. Rendered once, then rendered again, and again, for every business on earth.

This is a short manifesto for the other kind. The handmade kind. The websites that feel like somebody made them.

---

**We believe the web is a craft, not a form to fill in.**
A website is not a contact form wrapped in a template. It's a piece of work that argues, on behalf of a real business, to a real person who has a real problem. Treat it as such.

**We believe a site should sound like the person who owns it.**
If you say *we hate chasing invoices*, the site should say that. Not *we streamline accounts-receivable processes*. Nobody sounds like that. Nobody should be asked to.

**We believe texture matters more than style.**
Serif vs sans is a taste. Line height, easing, a margin that breathes — those are craft. The style is a choice. The craft is the thing visitors feel without being able to name.

**We believe a bespoke site is not a luxury.**
It is a small-business website that was actually made for your business. Everything else is a rented room with a generic print on the wall.

**We believe the best hover state is the one nobody notices consciously.**
If it makes you say *ooh*, it's too much. If it makes you stay on the page a half-second longer, it's just right.

**We believe motion should feel like weight, not like code.**
Things on the page should slow down and speed up the way things in the world do. Linear easing is the sound of a template. Easing with physics is the sound of someone who cared.

**We believe no website should be a dark-mode landing page with a purple gradient.**
Unless it's a deliberate choice for a specific reason. Ninety-nine times out of a hundred, it is not.

**We believe constraints are the point.**
*£0 per month to host. £12 a year for the domain. One month to ship.* These aren't the small print. They're the reasons the thing gets made well. The cheap, fast, slow-to-change web is the honest web.

**We believe a website is a long-form argument.**
Every scroll is a paragraph. Every section has a job. Every pause is on purpose. If a section isn't earning its place, delete it. Most sites have a confident fifth of the copy they started with.

**We believe the best websites feel a little bit alive.**
You can't fake this one. It's the sum of the nine above. A website that's been thought about, typeset, cared for, cut back, cared for again, shipped, and then cared for once more. That's the kind worth making. That's the kind worth paying for. That's the kind I try to make.
